Loft Insulation

💡 Save up to £690/year on energy bills

Loft insulation is one of the cheapest, quickest, and most impactful improvements you can make to your home. If your loft has less than 270mm of insulation — or none at all — you could be losing a significant amount of heat and paying far more than you need to on your energy bills.

For most properties, installing or topping up loft insulation to the recommended 270mm depth can be completed in just a few hours and will have an immediate impact on your EPC rating and your heating costs.

  • Reduces heat loss through the roof by up to 25%
  • Can improve your EPC by one full band or more
  • Payback period typically 1–2 years
  • Suitable for the vast majority of properties with accessible lofts
  • May be fully funded through ECO4 or the Great British Insulation Scheme for eligible households
  • Improves comfort throughout the home, not just in winter

Heating System Upgrades

💡 Save up to £580/year replacing an old gas boiler

Your heating system has a greater impact on your EPC rating than almost any other single factor. Whether you're replacing an ageing gas boiler with a modern condensing unit, installing a heat pump, or upgrading to a more efficient heating control system, the results can be dramatic.

Our assessors identify exactly what your current system is costing you and which upgrade path will give you the best improvement for your property type and budget — from simple boiler replacements to full heat pump installations.

  • Modern condensing boilers are up to 94% efficient vs. 60–70% for older models
  • Air source heat pumps can deliver 3 units of heat per unit of electricity consumed
  • Smart heating controls and thermostatic radiator valves can save 10–15% on bills alone
  • Heat pumps qualify for the Boiler Upgrade Scheme grant (currently £7,500)
  • Under-floor heating and heat pump combinations ideal for well-insulated properties
  • Biomass boilers may be eligible for Renewable Heat Incentive payments

Solar Panel Installations

💡 Average saving of £1,000+/year with a 4kW system

Solar photovoltaic (PV) panels convert daylight into electricity that can power your home directly, dramatically reducing your reliance on the grid. Even in the UK's variable climate, a well-positioned solar installation can generate a significant proportion of a home's annual energy needs.

When combined with a battery storage system, excess energy generated during the day can be stored and used in the evenings — maximising your savings and pushing your EPC rating towards A or B. Solar installations are particularly powerful for homes that already have good insulation and efficient heating, as they can tip a C-rated home into the B band.

  • A typical 4kW domestic system generates around 3,400kWh per year
  • Smart Export Guarantee (SEG) allows you to sell surplus electricity back to the grid
  • Battery storage increases self-consumption from ~30% to ~70%+
  • Solar thermal panels can heat your hot water for free during summer months
  • Panels are typically warranted for 25 years with minimal maintenance required
  • Adds significant value to your property and improves saleability

Wall Insulation

💡 Save up to £395/year with cavity wall insulation

Walls are responsible for around a third of all heat loss in an uninsulated property. The solution depends on your property's construction — modern homes built after the 1920s typically have cavity walls, while older and traditional properties often have solid walls that require a different approach.

Our assessors can identify your wall construction type during an EPC assessment and recommend the most appropriate and cost-effective solution, whether that's cavity fill, internal wall insulation (IWI), or external wall insulation (EWI).

  • Cavity wall insulation: quick to install, highly cost-effective, minimal disruption
  • External wall insulation improves aesthetics and weatherproofing as well as energy performance
  • Internal wall insulation suitable where external appearance must be preserved
  • Particularly impactful for pre-1919 solid-wall properties with poor baseline ratings
  • May qualify for full funding under ECO4 for eligible households
  • Must be assessed carefully in traditionally constructed buildings to avoid moisture issues

Double & Triple Glazing

💡 Save up to £195/year replacing single glazing

Single-glazed windows are one of the most significant sources of heat loss and cold draughts in older properties. Modern double-glazed units are dramatically more efficient, and triple glazing offers the highest thermal performance currently available for domestic properties.

For listed buildings and properties in conservation areas where traditional glazing must be retained, secondary glazing can provide many of the same benefits while preserving the original windows — our assessors understand these constraints and can advise on the best approach.

  • Modern double glazing is up to three times more thermally efficient than single glazing
  • Draught-proofing alone can save £60–£100 per year at minimal cost
  • Low-emissivity (low-e) glass coating further reduces heat loss
  • Secondary glazing available for listed and heritage properties
  • Glazing upgrades improve acoustic insulation as well as thermal performance
  • Most impactful when combined with other measures as part of a whole-house approach

Ventilation & Air Quality

⚠️ Awaab's Law & the Renters' Rights Act

Awaab's Law — named after two-year-old Awaab Ishak who died from prolonged exposure to mould — came into force for social housing in 2024 and is being extended to the private rented sector. Landlords will be legally required to investigate and resolve damp and mould hazards within strict timeframes. Adequate ventilation is the most effective preventative measure.

As modern insulation and draught-proofing reduce natural air infiltration, controlled ventilation becomes essential. A well-ventilated home removes moisture-laden air from kitchens and bathrooms before it can condense on cold surfaces — preventing the damp and mould that damages property and harms occupant health.

Our assessors assess your current ventilation provision as part of a full energy assessment and identify gaps — from missing window trickle vents to inadequate extraction in bathrooms and kitchens.

  • Trickle vents in windows — passive background ventilation that maintains continuous fresh air without draughts or heat loss; often missing in older replacement windows
  • Bathroom extraction fans — removes humid air at source; intermittent fans activate with the light, continuous fans run at low speed with boost mode
  • Kitchen extraction — cooker hoods and extractor fans prevent cooking moisture from migrating through the property
  • Mechanical Ventilation with Heat Recovery (MVHR) — whole-house system that continuously exchanges stale air for fresh air while recovering up to 90% of the heat; ideal for highly insulated new builds and retrofits
  • Positive Input Ventilation (PIV) — loft-mounted unit that gently pressurises the dwelling with fresh, filtered air; highly effective for condensation problems in existing homes
  • Damp and mould assessment — we can identify the root cause of moisture problems and recommend the correct ventilation solution
🏠 What Landlords Need to Know

Under the Housing Health and Safety Rating System (HHSRS), damp and mould is already classified as a Category 1 hazard — the most serious. Landlords with inadequately ventilated properties face improvement notices, fines, and rent repayment orders. Awaab's Law will tighten timescales further for both social and private landlords.

A retrofit assessment or EPC from SAS Home Energy will flag ventilation deficiencies and provide the documented evidence base you need to demonstrate compliance and prioritise works.

Roof Repairs & Draught Proofing

💡 Draught proofing saves up to £125/year — minimal cost to install

There's little point investing in high-quality insulation if the roof above it is letting in water, or if warm air is leaking out through gaps around doors, floors, and pipework. Addressing the building fabric first is essential — and with access to trusted roofing contractors, SAS Home Energy can help you tackle both assessment and remediation in one joined-up process.

🏗️ Trusted Roofing Contractors

We work with experienced local roofers who can carry out everything from minor tile repairs and repointing to full roof replacements and flat roof renewals. Getting roof issues resolved before loft insulation is installed protects your investment and ensures the insulation performs as designed.

Draught proofing is one of the most overlooked improvements — yet it delivers one of the fastest payback periods of any measure. Gaps around doors, windows, loft hatches, floorboards, pipework penetrations, and fireplaces can all be sealed at low cost with an immediate impact on comfort and bills.

  • Roof inspections & repairs — missing or slipped tiles, damaged flashing, blocked gutters, and failed pointing are identified and remediated before they compromise your insulation or structure
  • Flat roof renewal — older felt flat roofs are a common source of heat loss and water ingress; modern GRP fibreglass and EPDM rubber systems offer long-lasting, well-insulated replacements
  • Chimney and stack repairs — cracked pointing, damaged flaunching, and open chimney flues are significant sources of both heat loss and water penetration
  • Door and window draught seals — self-adhesive foam or brush strips on external doors and window frames eliminate cold air infiltration at minimal cost
  • Loft hatch insulation & sealing — an unsealed loft hatch can lose more heat than an uninsulated wall; fitting a draught-sealed, insulated hatch cover is quick and inexpensive
  • Floorboard and skirting gap sealing — suspended timber ground floors can account for 10–15% of draughts in older properties; filling gaps with flexible sealant makes a noticeable difference
  • Fireplace and chimney balloons — unused fireplaces lose significant heat up the flue; a chimney balloon or draught excluder stops cold air entering and warm air escaping
  • Pipework and cable penetrations — gaps around pipes passing through walls and floors are easily sealed with fire-rated expanding foam or collars
🔑 The "Fabric First" Principle

PAS 2035 — the UK standard for domestic retrofit — requires a "fabric first" approach. This means ensuring the roof, walls, and floors are in good condition and properly sealed before upgrading heating systems or installing renewables. An EPC or retrofit assessment from SAS Home Energy identifies fabric deficiencies as part of the process, giving you a clear, prioritised action plan.

A range of government-backed schemes can significantly reduce or even eliminate the cost of energy improvements. A current EPC is often a requirement to access these grants.

🏠

ECO4 Scheme

The Energy Company Obligation (ECO4) requires energy suppliers to fund insulation and heating upgrades for low-income and fuel-poor households. Fully funded in many cases.

Insulation & Heating
🇬🇧

Great British Insulation Scheme

Provides funding for single insulation measures — typically loft or cavity wall insulation — to households in lower Council Tax bands or those receiving qualifying benefits.

Insulation
🌡️

Boiler Upgrade Scheme

A £7,500 government grant towards the installation of an air source or ground source heat pump, reducing the upfront cost of switching away from gas or oil heating.

Smart Export Guarantee

Requires energy suppliers to pay you for surplus solar electricity exported to the grid. Rates vary by supplier — typically 4–15p per kWh.
