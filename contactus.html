Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does an EPC survey take?
Most domestic EPC assessments take between 30 and 60 minutes, depending on the size and complexity of the property. Larger or older properties may take a little longer.
How quickly will I receive my certificate?
Your EPC will be lodged on the national register and emailed to you within 24–48 hours of your assessment in the vast majority of cases.
Do I need to be present during the survey?
Someone over 18 should be present to provide access and accompany the assessor. Agents and property managers can attend on behalf of owners.
How long is an EPC valid for?
An Energy Performance Certificate is valid for 10 years. You can check whether a valid EPC already exists for your property on the government's register at epcregister.com.
Can you assess a listed or period property?
Yes — we specialise in older and traditionally constructed properties. Our assessors are experienced in evaluating solid wall construction, historic glazing, and other heritage features fairly and accurately.
What do I need to have ready for the assessment?
Access to all rooms and the loft (if applicable) is helpful, along with any documentation about insulation, boiler age, or heating systems. Don't worry if you're unsure — our assessor will guide you through it.
